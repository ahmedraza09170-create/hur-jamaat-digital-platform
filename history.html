A Legacy of Sacrifice

From Spiritual Leadership to Revolutionary Struggle — Explore the Journey of the Hur Community and their Custodians in Sindh.

The Gaddi of Pir Pagaro

The Gaddi of Pir Pagaro has long been a lighthouse of social justice in Sindh. The "Hurs" (The Free Ones) emerged as a disciplined force, dedicated to the defense of their faith and homeland, upholding spiritual values while guiding the community toward progress.

1819

Syed Muhammad Rashid Shah (Rozay Dhani)

Spiritual forerunner of the Hur Jamaat. His shrine laid the ideological foundation of the Qadiriyya Rashidiya order in Sindh.

1831

Syed Sibghatullah Shah I

First to bear the title Pir Pagaro. Initiated organized resistance against British colonial rule after the annexation of Sindh.

1847

Pir Syed Ali Gohar Shah Rashidi I

A respected Sufi poet and spiritual guide who strengthened the Hur Jamaat during early British consolidation.

1880

Syed Hizbullah Shah

Formally organized the Hur Jamiat and led armed resistance, transforming the movement into a structured force.

1896–1921

Syed Ali Gohar Shah II & Syed Shah Mardan Shah I

Period of consolidation and survival as British repression intensified against the Hur community.

1941–1943

Syed Sibghatullah Shah Rashdi II (Soreh Badshah)

Led the most intense anti-colonial uprising. Advocated Hindu-Muslim unity and was executed by the British in 1943.

1952–2012

Syed Shah Mardan Shah II

Restored after independence. Played a major political role and led Hur Mujahideen support during the 1965 and 1971 wars.

2012–Present

Sibghatullah Shah Rashdi III (Raja Saein)

Current Pir Pagaro. Leads the Jamaat and the GDA, combining spiritual leadership with modern political and social reform.

Political Leadership of Pir Pagaro

7th Pir Pagaro: Syed Shah Mardan Shah II (1952–2012)

Led the Pakistan Muslim League-Functional (PML-F), restoring Hur influence after independence. Mobilized the Hur Mujahideen during the 1965 India-Pakistan war. A major political figure in rural Sindh, balancing spiritual guidance with active politics.

8th Pir Pagaro: Sibghatullah Shah Rashdi III (Raja Saein) (2012–Present)

Heads the PML-F and formed the Grand Democratic Alliance (GDA) for greater influence in Sindh. Advocates girls’ education, environmental initiatives, and women’s property rights according to Shariat.

Political Evolution of the Hur Leadership
  • Earlier: PML-F under the 7th Pir focused on rural Sindh and spiritual-political guidance.
  • Now: GDA under the 8th Pir works on alliances, election reforms, and civic initiatives.
  • The Hur community continues balancing spiritual authority with active political engagement.
Social & Welfare Initiatives
  • Promotion of girls’ education and women empowerment.
  • Environmental programs like tree plantation drives.
  • Community guidance and dispute resolution following Shariat.
